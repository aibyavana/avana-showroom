00 / Before You Start

How this works.

This kit uses Sidekick the AI assistant already built into your Shopify admin. No new tools, no technical skill. If you can copy and paste, you can run this audit. Read these five rules first; the chat rule in particular will save you hours.

1
In your Shopify admin, open Sidekick (top bar, or search "Sidekick"). It reads your live store data and answers questions about it.
2
Work through the categories in order they're sequenced biggest-leak-first. Paste each prompt one at a time and wait for the full answer.
3
One fix = one chat. When Sidekick finishes a diagnosis it will often offer to make the fix. When you say yes, do it in a dedicated chat for that one fix start a fresh Sidekick chat and name it for the fix (e.g. "Fix · Meta titles"). That way you can return to that exact thread to run, pause, or re-run it without losing your place. Don't pile multiple fixes into one chat they get tangled.
4
Some checks happen outside Sidekick (robots.txt, AI search visibility). They're marked and take two minutes each.
5
Each category ends with a scorecard rate yourself 1-10 before you fix, and again after. That before/after is your proof the work mattered.
One honest note on accuracy
Sidekick samples large catalogs (often ~250 products at a time) because of data limits. Where that happens, treat the result as directional the pattern is real even if the exact count isn't. For a precise count, export your full catalog as CSV (Products → Export → All products). We flag every prompt where this matters.
01 / Category One · Biggest Lever

Checkout & cart recovery.

For most stores this is the single biggest pool of recoverable money revenue that reached the doorstep and left. Start here because the dollar figure you find is the one that makes everything else feel urgent.

Prompt 1.1 · The hook
Abandonment rate & lost revenue
Paste into Sidekick
For full-year 2025, what percentage of checkout sessions were abandoned, and how many sessions is that? Using my 2025 average order value, estimate the total revenue value of those abandoned checkouts, and estimate how much I could recover at a conservative 10% recovery rate.
What to look for
Anything above ~60% abandonment is high and means real recoverable money. This dollar figure is usually the most motivating number in the entire audit.
What to do
Write down the number. If you don't have a clean 3-step abandoned-checkout sequence, this is your fastest, highest-dollar fix even 10% recovery typically pays for itself many times over.
Prompt 1.2
Is a recovery flow even running?
Paste into Sidekick
Do I have an active abandoned-checkout recovery flow (email or SMS)? When was it last edited, is it enabled, and how many recovery messages does it send? If I have more than one cart or checkout flow, tell me whether they overlap and might be firing on the same customer at once.
[ FIX ]
If Sidekick offers to build or fix the flow open a new chat named "Fix · Checkout recovery" and do it there, so you can return to that thread to test and refine the sequence.
What to look for
No active flow at all (the worst and most common case). Or multiple overlapping cart flows that confuse customers and dilute recovery.
What to do
Consolidate into one clean 3-step sequence: reminder (1hr) · social proof/urgency (24hr) · incentive (48-72hr). Kill the duplicates.
Score · Checkout & Cart RecoveryFlow active, sequenced, no overlap, abandonment recovering?

02 / Category Two · The Door

Funnel & conversion.

Cart recovery recaptures money at the door. This widens the door. If most visitors never even add to cart, you're paying to bring people in and losing them before they engage and a sudden drop here often means something quietly broke.

Prompt 2.1
The full funnel, stage by stage
Paste into Sidekick
For 2025, walk me through my conversion funnel stage by stage: what percentage of sessions reached add-to-cart, reached checkout, and completed a purchase? What's my overall conversion rate? Tell me which single stage has the biggest drop-off.
What to look for
An overall conversion rate well under 1% with most loss at the session to add-to-cart stage means a top-of-funnel problem (findability, product clarity, page experience), not a checkout problem.
What to do
If the biggest drop is before add-to-cart, the fix is product clarity and navigation, not checkout. Note the weakest stage that's where to focus.
Prompt 2.2 · Urgent check
Did something recently break?
Paste into Sidekick
Compare my checkout sessions originating from collection pages and product pages over the last few months. Have they dropped sharply versus the prior period? Are there any sudden week-over-week declines in add-to-cart or checkout sessions from specific regions or page types that started recently?
Why this matters
A sharp, recent drop in checkout sessions from collection/product pages often means a theme update or navigation change broke something a buy button, a link, a mobile layout. This is urgent and fixable, and it can crater conversion overnight.
What to look for
A sudden 80%+ decline in sessions from a specific page type, or a region declining several weeks in a row. Sudden = something changed, not gradual market drift.
What to do
If you see a sharp recent drop, check your theme edit history and recent app installs immediately. Test the buy flow on mobile and desktop yourself before optimizing anything else.
Score · Funnel / ConversionHealthy add-to-cart rate, no broken-funnel red flags?

03 / Category Three · Margin

Discount margin.

Not a revenue leak a margin leak. Every discount dollar given to a customer who'd have paid full price is pure profit gone. This is the leak founders almost never measure.

Prompt 3.1
Total discount erosion
Paste into Sidekick
In 2025, what total dollar amount did discounts reduce my gross sales by, and what percentage of gross sales is that? Break it down by discount code so I can see which codes drove the most reduction.
What to look for
Total discount as a share of gross sales. Above ~10% is worth scrutiny. Which specific codes eat the most especially any always-on or sitewide codes.
What to do
Identify codes that are always available those are the ones subsidizing buyers who'd convert anyway. Note them for the incrementality check next.
Prompt 3.2
Incrementality: are discounts earning their keep?
Paste into Sidekick
For my most-used discount codes, what is the average order value of orders that used a discount versus orders that didn't? Are discounted orders bringing in new customers, or mostly being used by existing/returning customers who already buy from me?
[ FIX ]
If you decide to retire or restructure a code open a new chat named "Fix · Discount [code]" so the change is isolated and reversible.
What to look for
Discounts mostly used by returning customers = you're paying loyal buyers to do what they'd do anyway. Discounts that pull lower AOV than full-price orders erode margin twice.
What to do
Shift always-on discounts toward new-customer-only or first-order codes. Reserve incentives for the abandoned-cart flow where they actually recover lost sales.
Score · Discount MarginDiscounts targeted, incremental, not subsidizing loyal buyers?

04 / Category Four · Spend

Paid traffic & attribution.

Before judging whether ads work, you have to know they're being measured. A "0% conversion on paid" reading is usually a tracking problem, not a performance one and you can't optimize what you can't see.

Prompt 4.1
Where traffic & revenue actually come from
Paste into Sidekick
For 2025, break down my sessions and revenue by traffic source: direct, social, search, paid, email, referral. How many paid sessions did I have and what did they convert at? Does the paid number look suspiciously low compared to my actual ad activity?
What to look for
A tiny "paid" session count next to a huge "social" or "direct" count usually means paid social is being mis-bucketed because UTM tags are inconsistent. 0% paid conversion on very low volume is a measurement artifact, not proof ads fail.
What to do
Don't kill ad spend based on this number. Fix the measurement first (next check), then judge performance on clean data.
Check 4.2 · UTM consistency
Outside Sidekick · 5 min
Open the link/URL settings in each ad platform (Meta, TikTok, Google, Pinterest) and check whether every campaign uses consistent utm_source and utm_medium tags. What to look for: ads with no UTMs, or inconsistent ones, get dumped into "Direct" or "Social" and disappear from your paid reporting. What to do: standardize UTM tagging across every campaign so paid traffic attributes correctly then your conversion data becomes trustworthy.
Score · Paid Traffic & AttributionUTMs consistent, paid measurable, sources accurate?

05 / Category Five · Trust

Returns & fit.

Returns cost money twice the refund and the lost trust. Concentrated returns on specific products almost always signal a fixable fit or imagery gap, not a quality problem.

Prompt 5.1
Return rate & worst offenders
Paste into Sidekick
In 2025, what was my total dollar value of returns and how many units were returned? Which specific products drove the most returns by dollar value and by unit count? Is my return rate high or low relative to fashion benchmarks?
What to look for
Whether returns are concentrated in a few specific SKUs (often maxi dresses, fitted items) versus spread evenly. Concentration points to fit/sizing or a gap between photos and reality. A low total is a genuine strength worth noting.
What to do
For repeat-return products: add detailed sizing guidance, more accurate imagery, fit notes. If returns are already low, mark this category strong and move on don't manufacture a problem.
Score · Returns & FitLow return rate, no concentrated problem SKUs?

06 / Category Six · Foundation

Product data & SEO.

The layer everything depends on. If search engines and your own tools can't read your products, nothing downstream performs and thin product data is a hidden cause of the funnel leak in Category 2.

Prompt 6.1 · Precise count
SEO metadata coverage
Paste into Sidekick
How many of my products are missing a meta title, a meta description, or both? Give me the exact count for each and what percentage of my total catalog that is.
[ FIX ]
If Sidekick offers to write metadata open a new chat named "Fix · Meta titles" and work through bestsellers there. Keep it as its own thread so you can batch through the catalog over multiple sittings.
What to look for
Most stores find nearly the entire catalog missing both. Shopify auto-generates from short titles that say nothing to search engines or AI.
What to do
Usually the #1 data fix. Bulk-write meta titles + descriptions (CSV export + import is fastest for the full catalog; Sidekick for bestsellers now).
Prompt 6.2 · Sampled
Description depth
Paste into Sidekick
How many of my products have descriptions under 150 words, and how many under 50 words? Show me the five shortest descriptions in my catalog.
Sampling note
Sidekick may scan only ~250 products. If the sample shows ~99% under 50 words, assume it's catalog-wide. Confirm with a CSV export.
What to look for
One-line or empty descriptions. AI and shoppers both need materials, styling, occasion, brand story thin copy means products don't get found or added to carts.
What to do
Set a 300-word target for revenue-driving products. Use your single best description as the template. Rewrite bestsellers first.
Score · Product Data & SEOMetadata present, descriptions rich and searchable?

07 / Category Seven · Discovery

AI & GEO visibility.

The fastest-growing discovery channel in fashion, and the one almost no brand under $1M is competing in. These checks happen outside Sidekick and take two minutes each.

Check 7.1 · Are AI crawlers blocked?
Outside Sidekick · 2 min
Go to yourstore.com/robots.txt. Search for GPTBot, ChatGPT-User, PerplexityBot, and Claude-Web. If any sit under a "Disallow" rule, AI engines can't read your store. What to do: if blocked, that's a direct fix your store can't appear in AI search until crawlers are allowed.
Check 7.2 · Structured data present?
Outside Sidekick · 3 min
Open Google's Rich Results Test, paste 3-5 product URLs, check whether product JSON-LD is detected. What to look for: Shopify's native schema only works when titles and descriptions are complete so if Category 6 scored badly, schema is likely broken too. What to do: completing metadata usually repairs it automatically.
Check 7.3 · The GEO visibility test
Outside Sidekick · 3 min
Ask ChatGPT and Perplexity a question a customer would: "best resort jewelry under $200," "where to buy handmade beach necklaces." Screenshot results. What to look for: whether your brand appears at all. What to do: invisibility here is the opportunity fastest-growing channel, almost no competition in your category yet.
Score · AI / GEO VisibilityCrawlers allowed, schema valid, brand surfaces in AI search?

08 / Category Eight · Retention

Email & lifecycle.

Your existing customers are your highest-intent, lowest-cost revenue. This is usually where the biggest immediate upside hides missing flows every comparable brand runs. Works for Shopify Email or Klaviyo; adjust the tool name in prompts.

Prompt 8.1
Active flow inventory & performance
Paste into Sidekick
List my active email automation flows grouped by type: welcome, abandoned cart, browse abandonment, post-purchase, win-back. For each, show open rate, click rate, and unsubscribe rate, and compare to fashion benchmarks (automations: ~40-55% open, 3-6% click, under 0.5% unsubscribe).
What to look for
Your best performer note it, it tells you what your audience wants. Welcome emails with unsubscribe over 1-2%. Flows with strong opens but near-zero clicks. Flows with almost no sends (broken triggers).
What to do
Double down on what works. Fix high-unsub welcomes. Refresh weak CTAs. Investigate any flow with near-zero sends.
Prompt 8.2 · The big gap
Missing standard flows
Paste into Sidekick
Compare my active flows against the standard DTC fashion set: 3-5 email welcome series, 3-step abandoned cart, abandoned checkout, browse abandonment, a 3-4 email post-purchase series (thank you, care/styling, cross-sell, review request), and a 2-3 email win-back. Which am I completely missing or only partially running?
[ FIX ]
Build each missing flow in its own chat "Fix · Post-purchase series," "Fix · Win-back," etc. One flow per thread so you can refine each without tangling them together.
What to look for
A thin post-purchase sequence is the most common big gap one thank-you and nothing else, leaving cross-sell, reviews, and replenishment unbuilt for your highest-intent audience.
What to do
Build post-purchase first highest certainty because that audience already opens. Then complete partial sequences into full multi-step flows.
Prompt 8.3
Retention economics
Paste into Sidekick
What is my returning customer rate, and what is the average lifetime value of repeat customers (2+ orders) versus my overall average order value? Roughly how much is it worth to convert a first-time buyer into a second purchase?
What to look for
Repeat LTV is usually 2-3x a single order confirm the multiple. A returning rate under ~30% is room to grow toward 40%+.
What to do
This number justifies retention investment. A post-purchase flow timed to 30-45 days after first order (peak repurchase intent) is the highest-leverage move.
Score · Email & LifecycleAll standard flows live, performing, retention growing?

09 / Category Nine · Hygiene

Catalog hygiene.

Messy tags and collections quietly break collection rules, filters, automations, and any AI layer reading your store. Unglamorous, but it's what makes everything else reliable.

Prompt 9.1 · Sampled
Tag taxonomy
Paste into Sidekick
Summarize tag usage across my catalog. How many unique tags exist? Which are used on only 1-2 products? Any products with no tags? Flag duplicate tags from inconsistent casing (e.g. "earrings" vs "Earrings"), misspelled tags, and leftover promo tags like "25% off" or "BLACK FRIDAY."
Sampling note
For 100% coverage, export all products as CSV and audit the tag column there. The sample still reveals the patterns.
What to look for
Case duplicates (earrings / Earrings / EARRING), misspellings used as live tags, stale sale tags, app-generated system tags inflating the count.
What to do
Pick one casing convention, bulk-standardize. Fix misspellings. Strip stale promo tags (ideally with a Flow that auto-removes them post-campaign).
Prompt 9.2
Collections & duplicates
Paste into Sidekick
Review my collection structure: how many collections exist and how many products in each? Flag empty collections, internal/operational collections that shouldn't be customer-facing, stale promo collections, duplicate or overlapping collections, and any two collections sharing the same ID. Separately, are there duplicate or near-identical product titles in my catalog?
What to look for
Backend collections exposed on the storefront ("OUT OF STOCK," "DO NOT PUBLISH"), stale sale collections, near-duplicates like "ALL DRESSES" vs "DRESSES," and a bloated total (100+) where ~40-50 would do.
What to do
Hide/delete internal collections, archive stale sales, consolidate duplicates. Target 40-50 clean customer-facing collections. Merge or differentiate duplicate product titles.
Score · Catalog HygieneTags consistent, collections lean, no duplicates?

10 / Category Ten · Targeting

Customer segmentation.

You can't send the right message to the right customer without the right segments. This also surfaces data-quality gaps that quietly undermine all your geographic and lifecycle targeting.

Prompt 10.1
Segment coverage & data gaps
Paste into Sidekick
How many customer segments do I have and what are they based on? Am I missing any of these high-value types: product-category buyers (jewelry vs apparel), lifecycle segments (new 0-90 days, active, lapsed 6-18 months, lost 18+), and new-vs-repeat beyond simple order count? Also: what percentage of my customers have no country or location data?
What to look for
Missing lifecycle and category segments. A high unknown-location percentage (often from guest checkouts) that quietly breaks all your geo-targeting and inflates or undercounts regional segments.
What to do
Build lifecycle and category segments. Close the location gap with a post-purchase survey or optional checkout field so geo-targeting is actually accurate.
Score · Customer SegmentationLifecycle + category segments built, location data complete?
